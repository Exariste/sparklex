How to Remove Tough Stains Effectively

Oil, turmeric, ink and sweat — the methods that actually work, and the mistakes that make stains worse.

Back to Blog

Every household deals with the same handful of stubborn stains, and most of them get harder to remove the longer they're left untreated. The good news: with the right approach, almost any everyday stain can be lifted without damaging the fabric.

The single biggest mistake is using hot water before checking what kind of stain you're dealing with.

1. Act before it sets

Heat and time are a stain's best friends. Hot water can cook protein-based stains like sweat, blood or egg directly into the fibres, and a stain left for a day is always harder to shift than one treated within the hour. As a first step, rinse the area with cool or lukewarm water and gently scrape off any solid residue before applying anything else.

2. Match the treatment to the stain

  • Oil and grease: Apply a small amount of dishwashing liquid directly to the stain, work it in gently with your fingers, and let it sit for 5–10 minutes before washing. Dish liquid is built to break down grease, which makes it more effective here than regular detergent alone.
  • Turmeric and curry: Keep the item out of direct sunlight until treated — sunlight can set turmeric's pigment. Pre-treat with detergent and a little water, and wash in cool water rather than hot.
  • Ink: Blot, don't rub. Rubbing spreads the stain further into the weave. Dab from the back of the fabric with a clean cloth so the ink moves away from the fibres rather than deeper into them.
  • Sweat and deodorant marks: Pre-treat with liquid detergent on the underarm area and let it soak for 15–20 minutes before a normal wash cycle.

3. Pre-treat, then wash as usual

Most stains respond best to a short pre-treatment followed by a normal wash — not extra detergent in the main cycle. Apply liquid detergent or a small amount of detergent powder paste directly to the stain, let it sit, then wash with your regular load using a quality detergent that's formulated to lift dirt without weakening the fabric.

4. Check before it dries fully

Always inspect the area before putting clothes in the dryer or leaving them to air-dry. Heat can permanently set any trace of stain that wasn't fully lifted, so it's worth a second wash rather than risking a stain that's locked in for good.

With a consistent, well-formulated detergent and the right first response, most stains never get the chance to become permanent.
