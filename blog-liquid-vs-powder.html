Liquid Detergent vs Powder Detergent

Same goal, different strengths. Here's how to pick the right one for your wash.

Back to Blog

Detergent powder and liquid detergent both clean clothes effectively, but they behave differently in the wash — and the right choice often depends on your water type, washing method, and the kind of stains you deal with most.

How they clean differently

Powder detergent typically contains bleaching agents and abrasive-style cleaning particles that are very effective on ground-in dirt, mud and everyday grime — especially in cold water washes where it has time to fully dissolve. Liquid detergent dissolves instantly, which makes it better suited to pre-treating localised stains like oil or grease directly on the fabric before a wash.

Liquid detergent — best for

  • Quick, fuss-free dosing with no powder residue on dark clothes
  • Pre-treating oil, grease and makeup stains directly on the fabric
  • Cold-water washing, since it dissolves instantly at any temperature
  • Delicate fabrics, where a gentler, fully-dissolved formula is preferable

Powder detergent — best for

  • Heavily soiled clothes — work wear, children's clothes, mud and ground-in dirt
  • Whites and light colours, where the bleaching agents help maintain brightness wash after wash
  • Larger loads, where the cost-per-wash is often lower
  • Hand washing, where a dissolved-powder solution can be worked directly into the fabric

There's no single "better" option — many households keep both and choose based on the load.

A simple rule of thumb

If you're washing by hand, dealing with heavy dirt, or want the most cost-effective option for large loads, powder detergent is usually the stronger choice. If you're using a washing machine on cold or warm settings, want to pre-treat specific stains, or prefer a residue-free finish on dark fabric, liquid detergent will generally serve you better.

Either way, the formula matters more than the format. A well-balanced detergent — powder or liquid — should clean effectively without being harsh on fabric or skin, and leave a fresh scent that lasts beyond the wash itself.
