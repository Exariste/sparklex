Smart Home Cleaning Solutions

A simple room-by-room routine — and the right product for each job — for a home that always feels fresh.

Back to Blog

A consistently clean home rarely comes from one big weekend effort — it comes from short, focused routines spread across the week, with the right product matched to each surface. Here's a simple way to structure it.

Kitchen — daily

Grease builds up fastest in the kitchen, so it needs the most frequent attention. Wipe counters and wash dishes immediately after cooking with a dishwashing liquid that cuts grease without leaving residue — letting grease sit makes it harder to remove and gives odours time to set in.

Floors — every 2–3 days

Floors collect dust, food particles and outdoor dirt brought in on shoes. A disinfecting floor cleaner, diluted as directed and mopped in regularly, keeps both hygiene and shine in check — and a fresh-scented formula means the whole room benefits, not just the floor itself.

Bathroom — 2–3 times a week

Toilets and bathroom surfaces need more frequent attention than most people give them — moisture accelerates buildup.

A clinging gel-style toilet cleaner applied around the bowl and left for a few minutes before scrubbing handles limescale and stains far more effectively than a quick splash-and-go. Pair it with a glass cleaner for mirrors, which dry streak-free and quickly.

Windows and glass — weekly

Glass shows dirt and fingerprints more visibly than almost any other surface, which makes it worth a dedicated weekly pass. A fast-drying glass cleaner sprayed directly on the surface and wiped with a dry cloth avoids the streaking that water-only cleaning often leaves behind.

Laundry — as needed, but consistently

Rather than letting laundry pile up, smaller, more frequent loads with a properly dosed detergent keep clothes fresher and reduce the chance of stains setting in. Pre-treat visible stains before they go into the machine, and choose powder or liquid detergent based on the load — heavier dirt suits powder, while quick, residue-free washing suits liquid.

The product shelf that makes it easy

A well-stocked cleaning shelf only needs five things: a detergent for laundry, a dishwashing liquid for the kitchen, a floor cleaner, a toilet cleaner, and a glass cleaner. Keeping one reliable, well-formulated option for each — rather than switching brands constantly — makes it far easier to build the kind of short, consistent routine that actually keeps a home feeling clean.
