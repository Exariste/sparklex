Tips for Cleaner and Healthier Clothes

Small habits that make a real difference to how your clothes look, feel and last.

Back to Blog

Clean clothes are about more than how they look — the way you wash, dry and store fabric directly affects skin comfort, fabric lifespan, and how fresh clothes stay between washes. A few small adjustments go a long way.

1. Sort before you wash

Separating by colour is well known, but sorting by fabric weight and soil level matters just as much. Heavily soiled items release more dirt into the wash water, which can redeposit onto lighter, less-soiled clothes in the same load. Keep gym wear, kitchen cloths and heavily worn items separate from everyday clothing.

2. Don't overload the machine

An overloaded drum doesn't let water and detergent circulate properly, which means clothes don't get fully rinsed — and detergent residue left on fabric is a common cause of skin irritation. Leaving enough room for clothes to move freely also helps them come out less creased.

3. Use the right amount of detergent

More detergent doesn't mean cleaner clothes — it usually means more residue. Follow the dosing guidance on the pack relative to load size and water hardness, and let the formula do the work rather than the quantity.

4. Wash inside-out for printed or dark fabric

Turning clothes inside-out reduces friction on the visible surface, which helps prints, embroidery and dark dyes last longer through repeated washes.

5. Dry away from direct, prolonged sun

A little morning sun helps; hours of direct afternoon sun fades colour and weakens fibres over time.

Sunlight is a natural disinfectant and helps clothes dry faster, but prolonged direct exposure breaks down dyes and fibres. Where possible, dry in a shaded, well-ventilated spot, or turn coloured clothes inside-out before hanging them in the sun.

6. Store clothes only once fully dry

Folding or hanging slightly damp clothes traps moisture, which leads to a musty smell and, over time, mildew. Make sure clothes are completely dry before they go back in the wardrobe.

None of these habits require extra products or much extra time — just a little more attention on wash day. Paired with a detergent that's both effective and gentle, they're enough to keep clothes looking, feeling and smelling genuinely clean.
